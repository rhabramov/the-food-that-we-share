Cold Carrot Cake with Cream Cheese Frosting

YIELD — Makes one 9‑inch cake

This carrot cake, without raisins or nuts, is dense, custardy, and just as good straight from the fridge as it is at room temperature. Refrigerating it gives it that almost fudge‑like texture that makes it feel like a midday snack disguised as dessert.

Ingredients

For the cake

  • Cooking spray, for the pan
  • 1¼ cups (180g) all‑purpose flour
  • 1½ teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon ground turmeric, cardamom, or ginger (optional)
  • 3 large eggs
  • ¾ cup (155g) light brown sugar
  • ⅔ cup (145g) sour cream or Greek yogurt
  • 1 pound (455g) carrots, peeled and grated
  • 10–12 Medjool dates (about 8 ounces / 225g), pitted and chopped
  • 4 tablespoons (56g) unsalted butter, melted
  • ¼ cup (50g) neutral oil or olive oil

For the topping

  • 8 ounces (225g) cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup (60g) powdered sugar
  • Kosher salt, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons toasted nuts, chopped (optional)

Preparation

  1. Heat the oven to 350°F. Line a 9‑inch cake pan with parchment and coat lightly with cooking spray.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, cinnamon, baking soda, salt, baking powder, and any spices.
  3. In another bowl, whisk the eggs, brown sugar, and sour cream until smooth. Fold in the carrots and dates.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry and mix just until combined. Stir in the melted butter and oil until you have an even batter.
  5. Scrape into the prepared pan and bake 40–45 minutes, until puffed, golden, and pulling away from the sides.
  6. Cool completely. For the best texture, chill the cake in the fridge until very cold before serving.
  7. For the frosting, mash together the cream cheese, powdered sugar, and a pinch of salt until smooth. Spread over the cold cake and top with nuts if you like.

Discussion

This cake keeps beautifully in the fridge for a couple of days, and the flavor only improves. It’s the sort of dessert that works just as well with coffee in the afternoon as it does after dinner.
